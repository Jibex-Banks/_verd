AgriScan AI – Project Overview

1. Problem Statement
Smallholder farmers in Africa face devastating crop losses (20–40%) due to pests and diseases, yet they lack access to 
expert extension agents. Smartphone penetration is growing, but the 60% usage gap (people living within network 
coverage who don't use mobile internet) means cloud-based AI diagnosis tools are inaccessible. Farmers need instant, 
offline, and trustworthy disease identification to take timely action.

2. Our Solution: AgriScan AI
An offline-first mobile app that lets a farmer take a photo of a diseased leaf and receive:
Instant disease diagnosis (on-device, no internet required)
Explainable AI heatmap highlighting the symptomatic regions that led to the decision – building trust and teaching 
the farmer what to look for.
Unique Pillars
Offline Disease Detection Runs a lightweight CNN model 
entirely on the smartphone; no

4. Technical Architecture
4.1 Mobile App (Offline-First)
Framework:  Flutter (cross-platform) or native Android 
(Kotlin) for better performance.
Key Features:  Camera access, image gallery, model 
inference, heatmap rendering, local storage of results.
Offline Capability:  All ML models and treatment 
guides bundled inside the app; no API calls needed for 
inference.4.2 Machine Learning Model
Base Architecture:  MobileNetV3-Large (quantized 
INT8) for disease classification.
Why: Squeeze-and-Excitation modules help focus 
on disease lesions; excellent performance on low-
end hardware.
Pest Detection (optional):  YOLOv8-Nano (INT8) for 
counting pests like Fall Armyworm.
Explainability:  Grad-CAM (Gradient-weighted Class 
Activation Mapping) to generate heatmaps.
Training:  TensorFlow / Keras.
Conversion:  TensorFlow Lite with post-training 
quantization (INT8) to reduce size and speed up 
inference.

and shows its work."
8. Risks & Mitigation
Limited African crop diseases in training data Use transfer learning; prioritise 5–10 most important 
crops for MVP (cassava, maize, beans).
Model size too large for low-end phones INT8 quantization reduces size by 4x; target <10 MB.
Heatmap computation too slow on device Use simplified CAM with pre-computed weights; 
optimise with TFLite GPU delegate if available.
Low accuracy on field images Augment training with field-like conditions (varying 
light, background).
Yam data gap Transfer learning from cassava; declare use of web-
scraped images if any.
